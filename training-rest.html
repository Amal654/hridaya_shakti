Training


Objective of the course:

* Theory to guide one’s professional practice to give the best service to our patients
* Conceptualize a couple/family using the Bowenian framework to help the patient/s to discover their authentic self to relate with spouse, children and other family members
* Therapist’s own personal and professional growth will be stressed throughout the course
* Application of Bowen’s concepts to therapist’s family-of-origin to understand one’s own dynamics in the family and how it might impact him/her professionally
* Application of Bowen’s theory to organizations and institutions
* Application of Bowenian approach to understand our contemporary culture
* Application of Bowen’s theory to the Indian context
* Understand the neuropsychological foundations of Bowenian concepts in emotional regulation
* Develop critical thinking as couple/marital therapists in Indian culture

Duration of the course:

* This course will be held for 14 weeks for about 3 hours on Sunday afternoon. Participants may need to give more time to view videos before some modules.
* If necessary another group may be opened for a weekday course. If there are participants the class will be on Friday 8 a.m. to 12 noon OR 3 a.m. to 7 p. m. Participants will be consulted before deciding a suitable time.
* Begins on: Sunday Module: May 2 – July 1, 2010. Friday Module: May 7 – July 9, 2010.
* Registration ends on April 10, 2010 *

Maximum number of participants: 15
