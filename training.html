Training

The center offers an Advanced Certificate Program in basic and advanced counseling skills, various techniques in psychotherapy and psycho-educational programs. This part-time program is designed for beginners and professionals who work in clinical settings, and/ who wish to develop a solid foundation in psychodynamic theories, CBT and family system theories. Well equipped with a collection of over 300 DVDs on various forms of skill training, therapies and other related aspects of mental health and illness, the centre facilitates students and professionals to learn from experts in the field.

Bowen’s Family systems approach

Murray Bowen is one of the foremost original thinkers in the field of family therapy. His theory represents the intellectual scaffolding upon which much of the mainstream family therapy has been erected. Bowen, the developer of the family systems theory, conceptualized the family as an emotional unit, a network of interlocking relationships, best understood when analyzed within multigenerational in historical framework. His theory emphasizes authentic self-development, healing intergenerational issues, and significance of the past, and the systems approaches that restrict one’s attention to the family unit as it is presently constituted and currently interacting. Bowen believed the driving force underlying all human behavior came from the submerged ebb and flow of family life, the simultaneous push and pull between family members for both distance and togetherness. He remained its major theoretician until his death in 1990.
