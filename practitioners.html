Practitioners

PJ Pancratius MSC is a Clinical Psychologist. He received his Bachelor of Arts degree at St. Joseph’s College of Arts and Science, Bangalore. Additionally he received Bachelor’s degrees in Philosophy and Theology from Dharmaram Vidya Kshetram, Bangalore. He completed his Masters degree in Counseling Psychology from De La Salle University, Manila, and earned a Doctoral degree in Clinical Psychology from Illinois School of Professional Psychology, Chicago. He has lectured and trained counseling students at the Master’s level at Bangalore University and Montfort College, Bangalore and conducted workshops for schools and companies in enhancing human resources and enriching their personal and professional lives. He practiced in Chicago and brings rich experiences to individualize treatment plan for clients in Indian settings. Pancratius’ work is influenced by Self Psychology, Object Relations, Attachment and Intersubjectivity theories; Cognitive-Behavioral therapy, Acceptance and Commitment therapy (third wave in CBT), Dialectical Behavioral therapy, Body-centered Psychotherapy, Family system theories and integration of interpersonal neurobiological perspective of mindfulness.
